DATA SECURITY IS

EVERYONE'S RESPONSIBILITY!

DO'S:

  • Be smart when browsing/surfing the Internet or clicking links
  • Lock your computer and mobile phone(s) when you leaveyour desk and/or office
  • Use caustion when entering passwords in front of others
  • Create and maintain strong passwords and change them every 90 days (We
    recommend a combination of lowercase & uppercase letters and special characters)
  • Change your password immediately if you suspect that it has been compromised
  • Report suspicious activity to the IT team to help minimize cyber risks
  • Protect personal computers and devices with anti-virus/anti-malware software when working remotely, and ensure the anti-virus software is updated

DO Not:

  • Allow others to use your login ID or password
  • Use the same password for every application
  • Store passwords on a piece of paper or other places that are easily. accessible
  • Open email or attachments if the sender is unknown or suspicious
  • Get caught by phishing attempts, which can occur via email,phone,instant
    message, sms,or social media
  • Provide information such as login IDs, passwords social security numbers, account
    numbers, etc. via unencrypted email
  • Leave your laptop or mobile device unattended while in a public place
  • Keep open files containing personal or confidential information
    on your desks or in an unlocked file.
  • Install unauthorized programs on your work computer
  • Plug in personal devices without permission from the IT team
